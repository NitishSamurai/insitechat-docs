# Human Support & Escalation

When the AI can't help — or a visitor explicitly asks for a human — InsiteChat hands the conversation to your team. Every escalation is tracked, your team can **reply directly from the dashboard** (web widget, WhatsApp, *and* Telegram), the AI auto-mutes while a human is engaged, and the same `conversation.escalated` webhook fires for every channel with a `channel` field so you can route on it.

## How Escalation Works

When a visitor escalates:

1. The conversation is marked **Escalated** with a timestamp
2. Email alerts go to every configured recipient
3. The conversation appears in the **Human Support** tab as **Open**
4. A unified `conversation.escalated` webhook fires (if configured) with the originating `channel` (`web`, `whatsapp`, or `telegram`)
5. The visitor sees a confirmation message on whichever channel they came in on
6. Your team can reply from the dashboard composer — replies are delivered back over the same channel
7. The AI auto-mutes on that conversation while a human is engaged (rolling 24h, refreshed on every agent reply)

## Escalation Triggers

Escalations can be triggered from any inbound channel — and we use a **smart multilingual classifier** to detect intent, not just literal keywords:

| Trigger | Where it lives |
|---------|----------------|
| **Inline "Connect to an agent" CTA** | After every bot reply on the web widget (when enabled) |
| **Persistent follow-up button** with type *Escalate* | Configured in the Embed tab → Follow-up Buttons |
| **Compact "Talk to a human" link** | Below the widget input box (legacy fallback, hidden when the inline CTA is on) |
| **Smart intent on WhatsApp** | Visitor messages your WhatsApp number with phrases like "this isn't helping", "talk to someone", "agente humano por favor", "मानव से बात करनी है" — keyword pre-filter or LLM intent classifier triggers handoff |
| **Smart intent on Telegram** | Same multilingual classifier as WhatsApp — visitors don't have to remember a magic keyword |
| **Manual take-over from the dashboard** | Any open conversation can be taken over from the Human Support dashboard, even if the AI was handling it cleanly |

## Smart Intent Detection

Visitors don't always say "talk to a human" verbatim. They say "this bot is useless", "i need a real person", or write in Spanish, Hindi, French — anything. Our handoff detector handles all of it.

**Three layers, in order:**

1. **Literal keyword pre-filter** — fast, free, English-only. Catches "talk to human", "speak to agent", "real person", "live agent", "customer service", etc.
2. **Non-ASCII bypass** — if the message contains non-Latin characters (e.g. Devanagari, Hangul, Arabic, Japanese), we **skip** the keyword pre-filter and go straight to the LLM. This avoids false negatives in any language.
3. **LLM intent classifier** — Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite runs a tiny prompt: *"Does this visitor want to speak to a human? Yes/No."* Cached by SHA-256 hash of the text so duplicate messages cost nothing.

<Note>
The smart classifier is available on **Scale** and **Enterprise** plans. Free / Starter / Growth plans still support escalation via the in-widget CTAs, persistent buttons, and dashboard take-over.
</Note>

## Reply from the Dashboard

Open the **Human Support** tab → click any escalated conversation → type your reply in the composer → hit **Send**. The visitor receives it on the same channel they came in on:

| Visitor's channel | How they receive your reply |
|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Web widget** | Widget polls every 4 seconds while open and renders new agent messages with a green accent + "A human has joined the chat" system bubble |
| **WhatsApp** | Delivered via your connected WhatsApp Business number (subject to Meta's 24-hour customer-service window — see below) |
| **Telegram** | Delivered via your connected Telegram bot |

### What happens to the AI while you're replying

The moment you take over (or send your first reply), the AI is **muted on that conversation** for 24 hours. The mute is *rolling* — every reply you send refreshes the timer. If you stop replying for 24h the AI auto-resumes; marking the conversation **Resolved** or clicking **Hand back to AI** clears the mute immediately.

The composer also shows a live countdown chip ("AI paused — auto-resumes in 23h 12m") so you always know where you stand.

### WhatsApp's 24-hour reply window

Meta only lets you send free-form WhatsApp messages within **24 hours of the visitor's last inbound message**. If you try to reply outside that window, the dashboard returns a structured error (`code: "wa_window_expired"`) with the inline message *"WhatsApp's 24-hour reply window has expired. The visitor must message you first."* Pre-approved template messages outside the window are on the roadmap.

Web and Telegram have no such restriction — reply whenever you want.

### Agent identity privacy

Visitors see the agent's display name (`User.name`) if it's set, otherwise just **"Support team"**. Email addresses are never exposed.

## Configuration

Open your chatbot → **Embed** tab → **Human Support** sub-tab.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable inline escalation CTA">
    Toggle **Show escalation CTA** on. After every bot reply the widget renders two buttons:
    - A *positive feedback* button (e.g. "That answered my question 👍")
    - A *request help* button (e.g. "Connect to an agent 👤")

    Tapping the positive button records a thumbs-up and dismisses the CTA pair for that message. Tapping request-help escalates the conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally replace smart follow-ups">
    Toggle **Replace smart follow-ups with escalation** on if you want the CTA pair to take the place of auto-generated follow-up suggestions. Off by default — both can coexist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize labels">
    - **Positive feedback label** — defaults to "That answered my question 👍"
    - **Request help label** — defaults to your localized "Talk to a human" string
    - **Confirmation message** — shown to the visitor after they escalate (defaults to the localized "Sent! The team will follow up soon.")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add email recipients">
    Enter up to **10 email addresses** that receive escalation alerts. The chatbot owner is always notified — recipients here are *added* on top. Press Enter or click **Add** to add an address; click the × on any chip to remove.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Customization**. Settings take effect on the live widget within a few seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The user-level **notify_escalation** setting in account preferences is a master kill-switch. If you turn it off, you stop receiving escalation emails on every chatbot — even if you're listed as a recipient.
</Note>

## Human Support Dashboard

Every chatbot has a **Human Support** tab that shows escalated conversations and triage metrics.

### Stat Strip

Four KPIs computed over the last 30 days:

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Total escalations** | Conversations that hit "escalate" in the window |
| **Unresolved** | Escalated and not yet marked resolved (highlighted amber when > 0) |
| **Resolution rate** | `resolved / total escalations` |
| **Escalation rate** | `escalations / total conversations` (with avg hours-to-resolve when available) |

### Triage View

A master-detail layout: a list on the left, the full transcript on the right.

- **Filter pills** — Unresolved / Resolved / All
- **Conversation list** — last message preview, escalation timestamp, message count, status badge
- **Transcript pane** — full message history with timestamps, formatted markdown for assistant replies
- **Mark resolved** / **Reopen** — one-click status toggle, instantly updates the stat strip
- **Export** — download the transcript as CSV for record-keeping or CRM ingestion

<Tip>
Email alerts include a deep link straight to the relevant conversation: `/dashboard/chatbots/<id>/human-support?conv=<conversation-id>`. Click it from your inbox to land directly on the transcript.
</Tip>

## Email Alerts

Each recipient receives a plain-text email with:

- Chatbot name
- Visitor's most recent question
- Escalation timestamp
- A link to the conversation in the Human Support dashboard

Recipients are deduplicated case-insensitively and capped at 10 (plus the owner). Invalid addresses (no `@`) are rejected at save time.

## Unified Webhook Payload

A single `conversation.escalated` event fires for every channel — web widget, WhatsApp, *and* Telegram. The new `channel` field tells you where it came from so you can route accordingly:

```json
{
  "event": "conversation.escalated",
  "data": {
    "conversation_id": "uuid",
    "session_id": "wa_15551234567",
    "chatbot_id": "uuid",
    "channel": "whatsapp",
    "escalated_at": "2026-04-22T10:23:45Z",
    "last_message": "this isn't helping, can someone real help me?",
    "message_count": 7,
    "trigger": "smart_intent"
  }
}
```

### Field reference

| Field | Type | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| `channel` | string | One of `web`, `whatsapp`, `telegram` |
| `trigger` | string | One of `cta` (in-widget button), `keyword` (literal pre-filter), `smart_intent` (LLM classifier), `manual` (dashboard take-over) |
| `session_id` | string | Channel-prefixed (`embed-…`, `wa_…`, `tg_…`) — useful for joining with your own logs |
| `last_message` | string | The visitor's most recent message — handy for routing rules ("if it mentions billing → finance team") |

<Tip>
Pre-`channel` payloads (web-only) are still backward-compatible — the field is **added**, never replacing existing fields. Existing webhook handlers continue to work; new ones can opt-in by reading `channel`.
</Tip>

See [Webhooks](/managing/conversations) for delivery, signing, and retry details.
