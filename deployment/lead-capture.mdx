# Lead Capture & Management

Capture leads from every chatbot conversation and route them to the surface your team works in — the dashboard, email, Slack, HubSpot, or any webhook endpoint. The lead module ships on every plan including Free, with no per-lead caps.

## Lead Collection

### Set Up the Lead Form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Embed tab">
    From your chatbot, click **Embed** → **Lead Capture**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a starting point">
    Either start from scratch or click one of the **10 industry templates** to pre-fill fields, timing, and intent keywords:

    Dental, HVAC, Legal, Real Estate, Automotive, Healthcare, SaaS, E-commerce, Consulting, or Reset (blank). Templates are starting points — every field is editable afterwards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure timing">
    Choose when the lead form appears. **5 timing modes** are supported:

    | Mode | When it fires |
    |---|---|
    | `at_start` | Immediately when the chat opens |
    | `after_messages` | After a configurable number of visitor messages (default: 3) |
    | `before_escalation` | When the visitor requests human assistance |
    | `on_intent` | When the visitor's message matches one of your intent keywords |
    | `on_no_answer` | When the AI couldn't answer (uses your fallback message path or a built-in catalogue of "I don't know" phrases) |

    For `on_intent`, you can configure up to **30 keywords** (case-insensitive substring match). Common buying-intent phrases: `pricing`, `demo`, `schedule a call`, `talk to sales`, `quote`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add fields">
    Three built-in fields ship by default — **Name**, **Email**, **Phone**. Toggle each on/off, mark required/optional, or reorder them with drag-and-drop.

    Add custom fields with any of these **6 input types**:

    - **Text** — single-line free text
    - **Textarea** — multi-line free text (max 1000 chars)
    - **Email** — with format validation
    - **Phone** — with country-code auto-detection (see below)
    - **Number** — numeric input
    - **Select** — dropdown with custom options
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Changes**. The form is live on every page running your widget.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Country Code Auto-Detection

Phone fields detect the visitor's country from their browser timezone and pre-fill the correct dial code. **24 timezone-to-country mappings** are built in:

| Region | Auto-detected codes |
|---|---|
| India | `+91` |
| North America | `+1` |
| UK & Ireland | `+44` |
| Europe | `+33` (FR), `+49` (DE), `+34` (ES), `+39` (IT) |
| APAC | `+81` (JP), `+86` (CN), `+852` (HK), `+65` (SG), `+82` (KR), `+62` (ID) |
| Oceania | `+61` (AU), `+64` (NZ) |
| Middle East | `+971` (AE), `+966` (SA), `+20` (EG) |
| Latin America | `+55` (BR), `+52` (MX) |
| Africa | `+234` (NG), `+27` (ZA) |

If the timezone doesn't match any of the mappings the field falls back to `+91` (the default for Indian customers) and visitors can edit it manually.

### Multilingual Forms

The widget ships with full lead-form translations in:

- **English** (`en`)
- **Hindi** (Devanagari, `hi`)
- **Hinglish** (Roman-script Hindi, `hi-en`)

Right-to-left layout is supported via the `rtl` flag in widget branding settings. Every label — field placeholders, the submit button, the success message — can be overridden per chatbot via `branding.localization` for languages we don't bundle.

### Auto-Captured Context

In addition to the fields you collect, every lead is automatically tagged with:

- **`page_url`** — the page the visitor was on when they submitted (truncated to 2048 chars). Stored under `custom_data.page_url`. Useful for attributing leads to specific landing pages or blog posts. You can override or replace this by defining your own `page_url` field.

The full chat transcript (last 100 messages) is attached to every lead automatically — see [Conversation Context](#conversation-context).

## Lead Management Dashboard

Open **Dashboard** → your chatbot → **Leads**.

### Status Tracking

Each lead has a status that you (or an automation) can update. Statuses are manual today — there's no AI-driven scoring yet.

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **New** | Just captured, not yet reviewed |
| **Contacted** | You've reached out |
| **Qualified** | Meets your criteria |
| **Converted** | Became a customer |
| **Archived** | No longer active |

### Tags

- **Up to 20 custom tags per chatbot**, in **8 colors**: gray, red, blue, green, yellow, purple, pink, indigo
- Auto-tagging: leads are tagged with the conversation-starter title (the AI-generated session label) when one is set
- Assign tags individually or in bulk

### Search, Filter, Star

- **Search** by name, email, or phone (debounced after 2 characters)
- **Filter** by status, starred, or any tag
- **Star toggle** per lead — a fast "this matters" pin

### Bulk Actions

Select multiple leads and run any of **5 bulk actions**: star, unstar, archive, unarchive, delete.

### Detail Panel

Click any lead to open the full panel — contact info, every custom field, tags, status, capture timestamp, and the **complete conversation transcript** (last 100 messages with timestamps) from the chat session that produced the lead.

### CSV Export

Download leads as CSV — every column you've configured plus the built-in fields, status, tags, and timestamps. Export respects your active filters, so you can download just the segment you need. Capped at **10,000 rows per export**.

### Soft Delete

Deleted leads are recoverable. Nothing is permanently removed without an explicit re-deletion from the archived/deleted view.

<Tip>
Ask for less up front. Collecting just an email reduces form abandonment by ~30% in our data — you can always follow up for additional details on the call.
</Tip>

## Notifications & Sync

Every new lead fires a single `lead.captured` event that lands in **all four** of these surfaces in parallel — pick whichever your team works in:

### Email Alerts

A formatted email hits the chatbot owner's inbox the moment a lead is captured (toggle in **Settings** → **Notifications** → "New lead").

- Add up to **10 additional recipient emails** per chatbot. Each address requires a one-click email-link verification before alerts start.
- Rate-limited at **20 emails per hour** to prevent inbox flooding on busy days.

### Slack Notifications

If you've connected a Slack workspace via the Slack integration, every lead is posted as a formatted block (name, email, phone) to your chosen channel. See [Slack integration](/integrations/slack).

### HubSpot CRM Sync

Connect your HubSpot account once and every lead automatically creates a HubSpot contact — `firstname`, `lastname`, `email`, `phone`. OAuth tokens auto-refresh on 401. See [HubSpot integration](/integrations/hubspot).

### Webhooks (Zapier, Make, n8n, custom)

Configure an HTTP endpoint and we'll POST a signed JSON payload (HMAC-SHA256, header `X-InsiteChat-Signature`) on every `lead.captured` event. The full payload shape — including `custom_data`, signature verification snippets, and retry policy — is documented in [Webhooks → `lead.captured`](/api-reference/webhooks#lead-captured).

Use this with [Zapier](/integrations/zapier) (6,000+ apps including Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zoho, Airtable, Google Sheets), Make, n8n, or any internal HTTP receiver.

<Note>
All four surfaces fire simultaneously from the same event. There's no "primary" sync — you can use one, two, or all four. Webhooks come with a built-in retry policy (3 retries with backoff at 1, 5, 15 minutes); the dashboard delivery log shows status codes and bodies for every attempt.
</Note>

## Spam & Abuse Protection

Two safeguards run on every lead submission:

- **Rate limiting** — `5 lead submissions per visitor IP per chatbot per minute`. Above that, the public lead endpoint returns HTTP `429 Too Many Requests`.
- **Duplicate detection** — the same email submitted within a `5-minute window` for the same chatbot returns the existing lead instead of creating a duplicate row.

There is no CAPTCHA / Turnstile in front of the lead form by design — friction kills conversion. The two safeguards above stop the most common abuse patterns without blocking real visitors.

## REST API

Programmatic access for everything the dashboard does:

| Method | Endpoint | Auth | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| `POST` | `/api/chatbots/public/{chatbot_id}/leads` | None | Submit a lead from any source (called by the widget) |
| `GET` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads` | JWT | List captured leads with filtering and pagination |
| `GET` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads/{lead_id}` | JWT | Fetch a single lead with full custom data |
| `PATCH` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads/{lead_id}` | JWT | Update status or fields |
| `DELETE` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads/{lead_id}` | JWT | Soft-delete |
| `POST` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads/bulk` | JWT | Bulk star/unstar/archive/unarchive/delete |
| `POST` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads/{lead_id}/tags` | JWT | Assign tags to a lead |
| `GET` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/leads/export` | JWT | CSV export, respects current filters |
| `GET` `POST` `DELETE` | `/api/chatbots/{chatbot_id}/lead-tags` | JWT | Manage the chatbot's tag set |

See [API authentication](/api-reference/authentication) for JWT details.

## Roadmap

The lead module today does **manual** lead qualification — you (or your CRM rules) move a lead between New / Contacted / Qualified / Converted / Archived. AI-driven lead scoring and automatic intent classification are on the public roadmap; track the [changelog](/changelog/overview) for release notes.

<Note>
Conversational field-by-field lead capture (collecting fields one-at-a-time inside the chat itself, instead of a single modal) is also on the roadmap. Today the lead form is a single panel that appears at the timing you configure.
</Note>
