## Overview

Deploy your InsiteChat chatbot as a Telegram bot. Visitors message your bot on Telegram and receive instant AI-powered answers drawn from the same knowledge base as your website chatbot — same training data, same quality.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Bot via BotFather">
    Open Telegram and search for **@BotFather**. Send `/newbot`, give it a name and username. BotFather will give you a **bot token** (looks like `123456789:ABCdefGHI...`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect in InsiteChat">
    Go to your chatbot → **Webhooks** tab → find the **Telegram** card. Paste the bot token and click **Connect Telegram Bot**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start Chatting">
    Your bot is live! Send it a message on Telegram at `t.me/your_bot_username`. It will respond using your chatbot's trained knowledge base.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How It Works

When a user messages your Telegram bot:

1. Telegram sends the message to InsiteChat via webhook
2. InsiteChat runs the full RAG pipeline (hybrid search + context retrieval)
3. The AI generates a response using your trained knowledge base
4. The response is sent back to the user on Telegram

All messages count toward your plan's monthly message quota — same as website chat messages.

## Features

- **Same knowledge base** — uses the exact same training data as your website chatbot
- **Conversation history** — maintains context across messages (10-turn window)
- **Auto-greeting** — sends your chatbot's greeting message on `/start`
- **Plan quota enforcement** — respects your billing plan limits
- **Lead + conversation webhooks** — fires the same webhook events as website chat
- **Smart human handoff + reply from dashboard** (Scale & Enterprise) — see below

## Human Handoff (Scale & Enterprise)

Telegram conversations escalate to a human just like WhatsApp ones do — and once they do, you can reply right from the InsiteChat dashboard.

### Smart intent detection (multilingual)

The same multilingual classifier that powers WhatsApp handoff runs on Telegram messages. Three layers, in order:

1. **Literal keyword pre-filter** — catches "talk to a human", "speak to an agent", "real person", "live agent", "customer service", and similar.
2. **Non-ASCII bypass** — non-Latin messages (Russian, Hindi, Arabic, Korean, etc.) skip the keyword filter and go straight to the LLM, so language never blocks handoff.
3. **LLM intent classifier** — Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite picks up frustration, implicit asks, and ambiguous phrasing the keyword filter misses. SHA-256 cached, so duplicates are free.

### Reply from the dashboard

Once a Telegram conversation is escalated, it appears in your **Human Support** tab with a "Telegram" channel badge. Open it, type your reply, hit **Send** — your message is delivered through your bot to the visitor's Telegram account.

While you're handling it:

- The **AI auto-mutes for 24 hours** on that conversation. The mute is *rolling* — every reply you send refreshes the timer.
- A live countdown chip shows when the AI will resume.
- Marking **Resolved** or clicking **Hand back to AI** clears the mute immediately.

<Note>
Telegram does **not** have a 24-hour reply window like WhatsApp does — you can reply to a Telegram conversation at any time after escalation, even days later.
</Note>

### Webhook payload

Telegram escalations fire the same `conversation.escalated` event as web and WhatsApp, with `channel: "telegram"`. See [Human Support & Escalation → Webhook Payload](/deployment/human-support#unified-webhook-payload) for the full schema.

## Disconnecting

Click **Disconnect** on the Telegram card. The bot will stop responding to messages immediately.

## Limitations

- Responses are text-only (no rich formatting like the web widget)
- No lead form — use a different channel for lead capture
- Bot must be messaged first by the user (Telegram policy)
- Inbound media (photos, voice, files) is ignored — text messages on the same thread still work
